When Should You Replace Your Water Heater?

Know the warning signs before an emergency strikes

üìÖ

Age Matters

Most water heaters last 8-12 years. If yours is over 10 years old, it's time to start planning. The serial number on your unit will tell you its age‚Äîor just call us and we'll help you check.

üíß

Warning Signs

  • Rusty or discolored hot water
  • Strange noises (rumbling, popping)
  • Leaking around the base
  • Inconsistent water temperature
  • Not enough hot water for your needs
  • Visible rust or corrosion on the tank
üí°

Energy Efficiency

New water heaters are significantly more efficient than models from 10+ years ago. You'll save money on your energy bills every month while enjoying better performance and reliability.

‚ö†Ô∏è

Safety First

An old water heater can leak, flood your home, or even pose a carbon monoxide risk if it's gas-powered. Don't wait for a catastrophe‚Äîproactive replacement saves money and stress.

Not sure if it's time? We'll inspect your current water heater for free and give you an honest assessment.
